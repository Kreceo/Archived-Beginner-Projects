Expert Advice

"You can now take advantage of the longest days in the season by getting as much time down on your allotment. It's important throughout the warmer months to make sure you are weeding regularly, watering and feeding your crops, harvesting successional sowing. I find short regular bursts of weeding helps keep on top of them by hoeing between rows and pulling out weeds as you walk up and down your plot. Make sure you keep plants watered during hot spells, making sure you water early in the morning or late in the evening ensuring the plants are taking up as much as they need. Feeding crops like tomatoes, cucumber and squash every couple of weeks will help keep plants health and crop production high. You will now be harvesting wonderful crops like salad leaves, radish, beetroot, rock and spring onions. To make sure you have a continuous summer supply, successional sow these items every couple of weeks in small batches fro a continuous supply. Make the most of those beautiful long summer days and the rewarding harvests from your plot.”

Kirsty Ward
Founder of My Little Allotment
